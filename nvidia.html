go back home

Ever since I built my first computer on my own from scratch at age 14 I always messed with the graphics configuration before even looking at the start game, it was not until I looked at my friends graphic configurations that I realized that this is not what most people do. After building my computer I spent the next two years looking in youtube and forums for ways to get the most out of my hardware when gaming, I was doomed to having to do way too much research and troubleshooting since I mostly play 3d first-person shooters and I really liked them 100+ frames per second, but my hardware and budget were on the lower end of the spectrum, so I resourced to benchmark and trying every possible config to be able to click heads comfortably.

Ever since I found about Nvidia's geforce now I fell in love with the cloud gaming technology since it could save so much time for an experienced user to skip the installation process and simplify the graphics configuration process by being able to just select preference for performance, fidelity or balance. When I found how convinient cloud gaming was and what Nvidia's hardware was able to do with ray tracing a couple of years later, I decided that I would do everything in my power to one day work at Nvidia and contribute to the cutting edge gaming technologies.

Today, 7 years latter at the time of writting this
